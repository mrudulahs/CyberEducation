What is Phishing

Phishing attacks are the practice of sending fraudulent communications that appear to come from a reputable source

Types of Phishing Attacks

One of the most frustrating things about this is that most people know what phishing is and how it works, but many still get caught out

How to Spot It

In order to prevent a phishing attack, it’s important to be able to recognize one. Phishing attacks typically have similar features that can be spotted in order to prevent personal information from being stolen

How to Prevent It

Your email provider’s spam filter may keep out phishing emails, but attackers are constantly looking for ways to outsmart the filters

What is Phishing

- A type of social engineering attack where hackers trick people into revealing sensitive information by posing as a legitimate institution
- This includes personally identifiable information, bank details, passwords, clicking on dangerous links or installing corrupted software onto their machines
- Usually done through emails that seem to be from a trusted sender, so the victim is tricked into thinking the content is safe

Types of Phishing Attacks

Account verification
- Appears to come from well-known company like Netflix
- Asks you to sign in to your account
- Link points fake website and asks for users credentials

Fake invoice
- Contains a document that looks like an unpaid invoice
- Claims services will be stopped if invoice is not paid
- Impersonating supplier/retailer

Tax scam
- Appears to come from government tax agencies
- Claims that you haven't paid the correct taxes
- Fix the issue before further fines

Cloud file sharing
- Contains link to what appears to be a shared file on Google Docs etc
- Asks user to sign in to view the file
- Link points fake website

How to Spot It

- No personalisation
- Spelling and grammar mistakes
- Requests personal information
- High urgency
- Fake email address/web link

How to Prevent It

- Don't clink on any links - type them directing into the browser
- Use two-factor authentication - less likely to lose personal data this way
- Don't disclose personal/financial information without checking the source
- Use unique passwords for different accounts
